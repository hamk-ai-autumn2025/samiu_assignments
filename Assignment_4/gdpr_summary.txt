**Summary of Sources on GDPR:**

- The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is an EU law effective from 2018, replacing Directive 95/46/EC, aimed at protecting personal data and privacy for individuals within the EU and EEA [FILE: gdpr.txt, gdpr.pdf].
- GDPR is based on principles of transparency, accountability, and data minimization. Organizations must explain how data is used and obtain valid consent where required [FILE: gdpr.txt, gdpr.docx].
- It grants individuals rights over their data, including access, rectification, erasure, and portability [FILE: gdpr.docx].
- The regulation recognizes data protection as a fundamental right, balancing it with other rights and freedoms [FILE: gdpr.pdf].
- Non-compliance can result in significant penalties [FILE: gdpr.txt].
- GDPR aims to harmonize data protection laws across the EU, ensuring the free movement of personal data and supporting economic and social integration [FILE: gdpr.pdf].
